Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Sustainable Development Goals are a set of interconnected goals designed to ensure a better and more sustainable future for all. The SDGs were developed by the United Nations in 2015 to be achieved by the year 2030.

 

 

Among the SDGs, Goal 3 is concerned with health and includes 13 targets. The Ministry of Health, Sri Lanka has recognized and finalized 13 core indicators based on the targets of DG Goal 3 and there are 38 health-related indicators under them.  Additionally, there are 8  more health-related indicators that do not come under  SDG3 making the total number of indicators 46.
